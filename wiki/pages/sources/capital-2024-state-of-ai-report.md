## Citation

Nathan Benaich & Air Street Capital. (2024). *State of AI Report 2024*. https://www.stateof.ai/

## Key Findings

1. Annual review of the most significant developments in AI research and deployment across 2024. Covers research advances, industry trends, safety, and policy.
2. **LLM capabilities** continued to advance substantially in reasoning, multi-step problem solving, and multimodal tasks (text + images + audio + code). GPT-4o, Claude 3, and Gemini 1.5 represent step changes in capability.
3. **AI in education and training** is identified as a high-potential but underdeveloped application: early evidence (e.g., Kestin/Miller) is promising, but most deployed tools have not yet been rigorously evaluated.
4. **AI safety and alignment** remains an active research frontier; concerns about deceptive behavior, goal misgeneralization, and catastrophic risk are documented alongside technical progress.
5. Compute costs continue to fall while model capabilities rise — the democratization of capable AI is accelerating and will reach more educational contexts globally.
6. **Agentic AI** (AI systems that plan and execute multi-step tasks autonomously) is identified as the next frontier, with significant implications for workflows including educational content creation and tutoring.

## Methodology

Annual industry report. Synthesis of published research, industry announcements, and expert analysis. Not original research.

## Relevance

Provides context for where AI capability stands in 2024 and what educational applications are emerging. Useful for situating AI-in-education research in the rapidly evolving technological landscape.
