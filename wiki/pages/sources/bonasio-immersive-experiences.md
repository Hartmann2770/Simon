# Immersive Experiences in Education

## Citation

Bonasio, Alice. "Immersive Experiences in Education." *(Publisher and year not specified in metadata.)*

## Key Findings

1. Immersive technologies (VR, AR, mixed reality, and game-based environments) are becoming more accessible and are entering classrooms with increasing frequency.
2. The educational value of immersive technologies is highest when deployed in a pedagogically consistent manner — technology without pedagogical intent does not automatically improve learning outcomes.
3. When correctly integrated, immersive environments can expand the curriculum by enabling experiences that are otherwise impossible: historical simulations, virtual field trips, and interactive scientific visualisations.
4. The argument is made that immersive technologies are not simply supplements to instruction but can fundamentally change the nature of the learning experience by making learners active participants rather than passive recipients.
5. Game-based learning and immersive technology share complementary affordances: both leverage intrinsic motivation, feedback loops, and situated problem-solving.

## Methodology

Practitioner/analyst document. Not a peer-reviewed empirical study. Synthesises existing research and case studies to make a normative argument for immersive technology adoption in education.

## Relevance

Provides a conceptual and advocacy-oriented framing of [immersive learning](../concepts/immersive-learning.md). Useful as context for the broader movement toward VR/AR adoption in schools, complementing empirical reviews such as [Garzón et al. 2019](garzon-pavon-baldiris-2019.md) and [Pomerantz](pomerantz-xr-teaching-learning.md). The emphasis on pedagogy over technology aligns with constructivist learning design principles.
