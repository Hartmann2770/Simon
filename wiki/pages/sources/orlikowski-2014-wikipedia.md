## Key Findings

1. Wanda Orlikowski is an organizational theorist and information systems researcher at MIT Sloan School of Management, known for her work on **technology in practice** — the study of how technologies are enacted in organizational contexts.
2. Her **technology-in-practice** framework argues that the properties of technology are not fixed by design but are continuously reproduced and modified through patterns of use in specific organizational contexts.
3. This connects to the activity theory and postphenomenological traditions in understanding how technologies mediate work and learning.
