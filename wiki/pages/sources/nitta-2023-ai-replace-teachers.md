## Citation

Nitta, C. (2023). Will AI replace teachers? With Merlyn Mind founder, Satya Nitta. *Venture to Flourish* [podcast]. Google Podcasts.

## Key Findings

1. The central argument: AI will not replace teachers but will fundamentally change what teachers spend their time doing — automating administrative and low-level instructional tasks, freeing teachers for higher-value relational and mentoring roles.
2. The teacher's irreplaceable role is characterized as **human connection, motivation, and contextual judgment**: AI cannot build the relationships, model prosocial behavior, or exercise the situated moral judgment that effective teaching requires.
3. Current AI classroom tools (like Merlyn Mind) are positioned as teacher **amplifiers** — handling logistics, answering routine questions, and managing digital resources so teachers can focus on students.
4. The transition requires both technical change (better AI tools) and cultural change (redefining what good teaching looks like in an AI-augmented classroom).
5. The conversation touches on equity: AI tools that amplify excellent teachers can expand access to high-quality education; but AI tools that compensate for poor teaching conditions without addressing root causes may simply mask systemic problems.

## Methodology

Podcast interview. Expert opinion/practitioner perspective. Not empirical research.

## Relevance

Accessible framing of the AI-as-amplifier vs. AI-as-replacement debate. Useful for contextualizing research on AI tutoring (Kestin/Miller) with practical considerations about teacher roles and classroom reality.
