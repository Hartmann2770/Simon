## Citation

Munz, C. (2024). Artificial intelligence and the future of learning. *Articulate* [webinar]. https://articulate.com/

## Key Findings

1. AI's potential in L&D (Learning and Development) is substantial but requires clarity about what problems it actually solves: speeding up content production, personalizing learning pathways, and providing scalable feedback are the best-documented use cases.
2. The distinction between **AI as efficiency tool** (doing existing things faster) and **AI as transformation tool** (enabling qualitatively new forms of learning) is important: most current implementations focus on efficiency; transformation requires rethinking learning design from first principles.
3. Learning and development professionals should build **AI literacy** as a professional capability — not to become AI engineers, but to be informed commissioners and evaluators of AI tools for learning.
4. The webinar highlights concrete use cases: using AI for rapid content development, for adaptive assessment, and for intelligent learning experience personalization based on learner data.
5. Human judgment remains essential for quality assurance, ethical oversight, and contextual adaptation that AI systems cannot reliably provide.

## Methodology

Practitioner webinar. Expert presentation, not primary research.

## Relevance

Practical perspective from an L&D tools provider (Articulate). Useful for understanding how AI is being deployed in commercial learning design contexts, and what practitioners are prioritizing.
