## Citation

Forneris, S. G., Tagliareni, M. E., & Allen, B. (2022). Accelerating to practice: Defining a competency-based curriculum framework for nursing education Part 1. *Nursing Education Perspectives, 43*(6), 337–341. https://doi.org/10.1097/01.NEP.0000000000000954

## Key Findings

1. The paper responds to a documented readiness-for-practice gap in nursing: novice nurses are graduating without sufficient preparedness for professional clinical practice, and traditional curriculum models are insufficient to close this gap.
2. A competency-based curriculum framework is proposed through a National League for Nursing (NLN) consensus process (Utstein-style meeting) involving nurse educators, practitioners, and researchers.
3. The framework centres on *outcomes* — what a graduate must be able to *do* — rather than on content coverage or seat time, aligning with broader competency-based education (CBE) principles.
4. Learning objectives in the framework are written as observable, assessable competencies mapped to specific practice contexts, moving beyond Bloom's Taxonomy toward performance-based formulations.
5. Curriculum design must integrate simulation, clinical practice, and didactic instruction in a coherent progression from novice toward professional practice readiness.
6. The framework explicitly addresses the theory-practice gap: content taught in isolation from clinical contexts fails to transfer; learning must be situated in contexts that mirror the performance environment.

## Methodology

Consensus-building study using the Utstein-style methodology (a structured expert-meeting approach originally developed for resuscitation research). A multidisciplinary group convened to build consensus on framework components.

## Relevance

Demonstrates how evidence-based instructional design principles (competency-based objectives, situated learning, theory-practice integration) apply at the curriculum level in professional education. Relevant to instructional design and learning objectives research.
