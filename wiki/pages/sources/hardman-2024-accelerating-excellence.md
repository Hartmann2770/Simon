## Citation

Hardman, P. (2024, April 4). Accelerating excellence? *Dr Phil's Newsletter, Powered by DOMS AI*. https://drphilippahardman.substack.com/p/accelerating-excellence

## Key Findings

1. Reports initial findings from research into the impact of AI on the speed and quality of instructional design, focused on Hardman's DOMS (Design of Meaningful and Systematic) learning design engine.
2. AI assistance can accelerate the design workflow for instructional designers, particularly for generating first drafts of learning objectives, assessment items, and content structures.
3. However, acceleration does not automatically equal quality improvement — AI tools designed without an evidence-based learning science foundation may produce faster but less effective learning designs.
4. The findings suggest that the most effective AI-assisted design occurs when AI handles lower-level generative tasks (content drafting, formatting) while the human designer retains responsibility for evidence-based learning architecture decisions.

## Methodology

Practitioner research report. Based on Hardman's observations from developing and using the DOMS AI platform. Not a controlled experiment.

## Relevance

Emerging perspective on AI's role in professional instructional design. Relevant to understanding how evidence-based design frameworks interact with AI-assisted production workflows.
