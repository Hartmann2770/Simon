## Citation

Thrift, N. (2005). *Knowing Capitalism*. SAGE Publications.

## Key Findings

1. Contemporary capitalism is fundamentally a **knowledge economy** in which the production, circulation, and manipulation of knowledge and affect is the primary source of value — not just goods and services.
2. Thrift develops **non-representational theory**: human action and social life are primarily enacted through practices, habits, and embodied know-how rather than through explicit representations and deliberate reasoning.
3. Capitalism's knowledge apparatus works through creating and circulating **soft capitalism** — management theories, consultancy ideas, business books, training programs — that shape how workers and organizations understand and perform their activities.
4. Space and place are not passive containers for economic activity but are actively produced through capitalist practices — geography is made through commerce, not just inhabited by it.
5. The chapter on **qualculation** (qualitative calculation that is embedded in everyday practice rather than explicit computation) is particularly influential — it describes how economic actors make decisions through embodied competence rather than formal rationality.

## Methodology

Theoretical and empirical analysis combining economic geography, science and technology studies, and cultural theory. Draws on ethnographic and interview research in corporate and knowledge-work settings.

## Relevance

Provides theoretical grounding for understanding how knowledge is produced, circulated, and valued in contemporary organizations. Relevant to understanding workplace learning, professional development, and the political economy of educational knowledge production.
