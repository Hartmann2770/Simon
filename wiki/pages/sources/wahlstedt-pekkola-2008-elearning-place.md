## Citation

Wahlstedt, A., Pekkola, S., & Niemelä, M. (2008). From e-learning space to e-learning place. *British Journal of Educational Technology*, 39(6), 1020–1030.

## Key Findings

1. E-learning systems are typically designed as **spaces** — structured around geometric and functional properties (modules, pages, navigation) — but effective online learning requires environments that function as **places** — social environments where learners feel at home and develop community.
2. The space-to-place transition requires three interacting elements: **space** (the technical infrastructure), **community** (social relationships and shared norms among participants), and **practice** (the activities and routines that develop over time in the environment).
3. Students in pure space-like e-learning environments report alienation, lack of motivation, and shallow engagement. The introduction of community-building features (discussion forums, social profiles, informal spaces) enables place-formation and deeper engagement.
4. The authors argue for intentional **place design** in e-learning: beyond organizing content delivery, designers must create conditions for community and practice to develop.
5. **Social presence** — the sense of other people being genuinely there — is a key affordance that transforms e-learning spaces into places.

## Methodology

Conceptual analysis combined with empirical case study of an e-learning platform in a Finnish university. Mixed methods: survey data on student experience plus qualitative analysis of participation patterns.

## Relevance

Directly applies space/place theory to e-learning design. Provides both theoretical framework and practical guidance for online course design. Widely cited in educational technology and computer-supported learning literature.
