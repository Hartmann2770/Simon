## Citation

Azi, J. R. (2006). Interactive child learning aid project (i-CLAP): Design and development of an indigenous instructional multimedia prototype for enhancing learning. Unpublished project/article. http://ijedict.dec.uwi.edu/viewarticle.php?id=175&layout=html

## Key Findings

1. The project addresses the design and development of an indigenous multimedia learning prototype for children in a context where Western-produced educational software does not reflect local cultural norms, language, or contexts.
2. The prototype uses culturally resonant content, local languages, and indigenous knowledge contexts to engage learners who are disengaged from decontextualized Western educational content.
3. Key design principles: **cultural relevance** (content drawn from learners' own communities), **multimedia integration** (combining audio, visual, and interactive elements), and **simplicity** (designed for low-resource settings).
4. The project illustrates how constructivist principles (learning through meaningful, culturally situated engagement) apply in non-Western educational contexts.

## Methodology

Design and development project report. Descriptive account of prototype development and design rationale.

## Relevance

Relevant to understanding how learning design must attend to cultural context and indigenous knowledge. Connects to situated learning theory and to discussions of educational equity in technology design.
