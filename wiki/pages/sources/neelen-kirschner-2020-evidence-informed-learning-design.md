## Citation

Neelen, M., & Kirschner, P. A. (2020). *Evidence-informed learning design: Creating training to improve performance*. Kogan Page.

## Key Findings

1. The book argues that L&D practice is disproportionately driven by fads, trends, and personal preferences rather than by evidence, and proposes a systematic framework for evidence-informed design.
2. Three questions structure the framework: (1) Is there a performance problem worth solving? (2) Can training solve it? (3) What does the evidence say about how to design that training?
3. Learning design decisions should always begin with a performance analysis — identifying the gap between current and desired performance — rather than jumping directly to content or format choices.
4. The authors synthesise cognitive science findings on retrieval practice, spaced repetition, interleaving, and feedback and translate each into practical L&D design implications.
5. The book explicitly rejects several popular L&D myths: learning styles, the 70-20-10 rule as a rigid formula, and the idea that learner preference equals learning effectiveness.
6. Evidence-informed design requires L&D professionals to develop research literacy: the ability to evaluate the quality and applicability of evidence rather than simply citing studies.

## Methodology

Practitioner book blending literature review, case examples, and design heuristics. Not a primary research study; synthesises cognitive science and educational research for an L&D professional audience.

## Relevance

The most comprehensive evidence-based guide for L&D practitioners currently available. Bridges the gap between academic learning science and applied training design. Directly relevant to instructional design, curriculum development, and workplace learning.
