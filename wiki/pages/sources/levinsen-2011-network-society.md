## Citation

Levinsen, K. T. (2011). Udfordringer i netværkssamfundet – digitale kompetencer og it i nye undervisningsmiljøer [Challenges in the network society – digital competencies and IT in new learning environments]. *Dansk Universitetspædagogisk Tidsskrift*, (11), 20–25.

## Key Findings

1. The network society places new demands on education to cultivate competencies defined internationally as necessary for 21st-century participation — digital literacy, collaboration, and adaptability.
2. Through a case study of an IT-didactic design master's program, the article argues that competency development requires a phenomenological exploration of learning processes — not just defining competencies as outcomes but understanding how they develop through experience.
3. A **social constructivist and materially anchored pedagogy** is proposed as the framework for IT-didactic design: learning arises through social negotiation and material engagement with digital tools in authentic contexts.
4. The case study demonstrates tensions and challenges when designing for competency development in technology-rich environments: students must simultaneously develop technical skills, reflective capacity, and subject-matter understanding.
5. Didactic design that supports competency development must go beyond content delivery to structure conditions for situated, reflective practice with technology.

## Methodology

Qualitative case study of a specific higher education course (IT-Didaktisk Design). Descriptive analysis with theoretical framing using constructivism and phenomenology.

## Relevance

Relevant to understanding how digital competency development requires not just new tools but new pedagogical frameworks. Connects constructivist pedagogy, phenomenological analysis of learning, and practical instructional design challenges in technology-rich contexts.
