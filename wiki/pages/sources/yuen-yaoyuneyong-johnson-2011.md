# Augmented Reality: An Overview and Five Directions for AR in Education

## Citation

Yuen, Steve Chi-Yin, Gallayanee Yaoyuneyong, and Erik Johnson. "Augmented Reality: An Overview and Five Directions for AR in Education." *Journal of Educational Technology Development and Exchange* 4, no. 1 (2011): 119–140. https://doi.org/10.18785/jetde.0401.10

## Key Findings

1. The paper provides a foundational overview of AR technology and identifies five promising directions for educational application: (1) AR books and print media, (2) AR gaming and discovery-based learning, (3) AR object modelling and spatial learning, (4) AR skills training and simulation, and (5) AR for personal information management.
2. AR enhances the real world with computer-generated content tied to specific locations and/or activities, creating a mixed-reality experience that differs fundamentally from fully immersive VR.
3. Mobile devices (smartphones and tablets) were already beginning to democratise AR access in 2011, making it more practical for classroom deployment than VR hardware.
4. The authors argue that AR's power lies in contextualisation: information is presented where and when it is most relevant, supporting situated learning.
5. Each of the five directions is illustrated with existing or prototype applications, demonstrating feasibility across educational levels (K-12 through higher education).

## Methodology

Conceptual overview and review article. Not an empirical study. Synthesises the AR literature as of 2011 and proposes a forward-looking taxonomy of educational applications.

## Relevance

This 2011 paper is an early and influential framing of [augmented reality in education](../concepts/augmented-reality-in-education.md). Its situated learning framing connects AR to broader constructivist learning theory. The taxonomy of five directions has been used by subsequent reviewers including [Majeed & Ali 2020](majeed-ali-2020.md) and [Diegmann & Schmidt-Kraepelin](diegmann-schmidt-kraepelin-ar-benefits.md) as a reference framework.
