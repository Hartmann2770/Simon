# A Review of Augmented Reality in Educational Applications

## Citation

Majeed, Zainab H., and Huda A. Ali. "A Review of Augmented Reality in Educational Applications." *International Journal of Advanced Technology and Engineering Exploration* 7, no. 62 (2020): 20–27. https://doi.org/10.19101/IJATEE.2019.650068

## Key Findings

1. AR provides a distinctive user experience through 3D virtual information overlaid on the real environment, enabling learners to interact with content that would otherwise be abstract or inaccessible.
2. The review distinguishes three main types of AR in education — marker-based, markerless, and projection-based — each with different technical requirements and pedagogical affordances.
3. Studies across multiple disciplines (science, mathematics, history, language learning) consistently report improved learner engagement and motivation compared to conventional instruction.
4. Knowledge acquisition outcomes were positive in the majority of reviewed studies, with STEM subjects showing particularly strong effects, likely due to AR's strength in spatial and 3D visualisation.
5. The review identifies challenges: technical limitations (device cost, battery life, tracking accuracy), the need for specialised authoring tools, and limited teacher training in AR pedagogies.
6. Accessibility and equity concerns are raised: AR benefits are unevenly distributed because access to capable devices varies by institution and geography.

## Methodology

Literature review. Systematic search of databases for peer-reviewed articles on AR in educational applications. Studies reviewed from approximately 2010–2020, covering K-12, higher education, and professional training contexts.

## Relevance

This review provides a more recent and comprehensive update to [Yuen et al. 2011](yuen-yaoyuneyong-johnson-2011.md) on AR in education. Its focus on types of AR and challenges to adoption complements the benefits-focused [Diegmann & Schmidt-Kraepelin](diegmann-schmidt-kraepelin-ar-benefits.md) and the quantitative meta-analysis by [Garzón et al. 2019](garzon-pavon-baldiris-2019.md). Directly relevant to [augmented reality in education](../concepts/augmented-reality-in-education.md).
