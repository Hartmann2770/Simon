# Augmented Reality as Multimedia: The Case for Situated Vocabulary Learning

## Citation

Santos, Marc Ericson C., Arno in Wolde Luebke, Takafumi Taketomi, Goshiro Yamamoto, Christian Sandor, and Hirokazu Kato. "Augmented Reality as Multimedia: The Case for Situated Vocabulary Learning." *Research and Practice in Technology Enhanced Learning* (year not specified in metadata).

## Key Findings

1. The paper reframes AR as a type of multimedia situated in authentic environments, applying Mayer's multimedia learning theory as a design framework for AR educational applications.
2. AR supports situated vocabulary learning by presenting words in context — superimposing labels and translations onto real objects in the learner's environment — rather than through decontextualised flashcard-style instruction.
3. A handheld AR system for vocabulary learning was developed and evaluated; results showed improved retention compared to a conventional flashcard condition.
4. The situated nature of the AR experience — seeing the word "chair" overlaid on an actual chair — exploits context-dependent memory effects, where encoding in a rich real-world context aids later retrieval.
5. The authors argue that AR's distinctive educational value is its ability to merge the learning content with the physical context in which that content is meaningful, enabling learning that is both multimodal and embedded.

## Methodology

Design-based research combining system development with a comparative evaluation. Participants were learners of a second language using either the AR system or a traditional flashcard system. Outcome measure: vocabulary retention test.

## Relevance

This study is important for [augmented reality in education](../concepts/augmented-reality-in-education.md) because it grounds AR design in established multimedia and situated learning theory rather than novelty. The situated learning angle connects to constructivist principles in the wiki. The context-dependent memory argument links to [retrieval practice](../concepts/retrieval-practice.md) — specifically to encoding specificity and contextual reinstatement as retrieval aids.
