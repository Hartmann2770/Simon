## Citation

Glover, J. A. (n.d.). The "testing" phenomenon: Not gone but nearly forgotten. *Journal of Educational Psychology* (exact year and volume not available in metadata).

## Key Findings

1. The testing effect — the finding that taking a memory test on material improves subsequent retention more than restudying — was documented in early twentieth-century research but subsequently fell into neglect.
2. Glover's work is among the early modern rediscoveries of the phenomenon and draws attention to it as an under-appreciated finding with educational implications.
3. The paper argues that the phenomenon is robust and practically significant despite the paucity of educational research attention it had received at the time.

## Methodology

Empirical and/or review paper (full details unavailable from metadata). The title indicates a focus on re-establishing the historical importance and empirical status of the testing phenomenon.

## Relevance

A historically important paper in the revival of testing-effect research before the major wave of studies by Roediger, Karpicke, and colleagues in the 2000s. It signals that the testing effect was periodically rediscovered but not consistently integrated into mainstream learning research until recently.
