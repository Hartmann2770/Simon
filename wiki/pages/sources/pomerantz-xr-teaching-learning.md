# XR for Teaching and Learning: Year 2 of the EDUCAUSE/HP Campus of the Future Project

## Citation

Pomerantz, Jeffrey. "XR for Teaching and Learning: Year 2 of the EDUCAUSE/HP Campus of the Future Project." EDUCAUSE, *(year not specified in metadata).*

## Key Findings

1. Year 2 of the EDUCAUSE/HP Campus of the Future project documented expanded institutional adoption of AR, VR, and mixed reality (collectively XR) across higher education campuses.
2. Faculty and instructors reported significant barriers to adoption: lack of technical support, unclear pedagogical frameworks, and difficulty fitting XR into existing curricula.
3. Despite barriers, institutions that piloted XR reported high student enthusiasm and perceived engagement, mirroring findings from experimental studies.
4. A taxonomy of XR use cases in higher education was developed, covering visualisation, simulation, field experience, and social learning.
5. The report recommends institutional investment in instructional design support alongside technology procurement — hardware alone does not drive pedagogical innovation.
6. Scalability and sustainability were flagged as ongoing concerns: many XR pilots remained siloed within single courses or departments.

## Methodology

Institutional survey and case study research across EDUCAUSE member institutions participating in the HP Campus of the Future project. Mixed methods: survey data on adoption rates and barriers, qualitative case studies of specific XR implementations.

## Relevance

Provides a macro-level institutional perspective on XR adoption, complementing the experimental studies of individual learning outcomes. The barriers identified (pedagogical frameworks, support infrastructure) are relevant to the practical application of findings from [Jensen & Konradsen 2017](jensen-konradsen-2017.md) and [Allcoat & von Mühlenen 2018](allcoat-muhlenen-2018.md). Connects to [immersive learning](../concepts/immersive-learning.md) and [augmented reality in education](../concepts/augmented-reality-in-education.md).
