## Citation

Binks, S. (2018). Testing enhances learning: A review of the literature. *Journal of Professional Nursing, 34*(3), 205–210. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.profnurs.2017.08.008

[Read PDF in Zotero](https://www.zotero.org/esaitch/items/RM5NBIDX/attachment/LV9QT5LZ/reader)

## Key Findings

1. Testing produces learning advantages superior to studying alone — the testing effect is well established and consistent across diverse populations and contexts.
2. Testing promotes superior retention, recall, and organisation of information within memory.
3. Testing is under-utilised as a pedagogical strategy by teachers and as a self-regulatory strategy by learners, despite strong evidence of its effectiveness.
4. Optimal testing conditions: (a) recall-format tests (rather than recognition), (b) spaced at intervals over time, and (c) accompanied by corrective feedback.
5. Educators should explicitly teach learners to use self-testing as a life-long learning strategy, particularly important in professional healthcare practice.

## Methodology

Narrative review of the testing-effect literature with a targeted focus on implications for nursing and professional education.

## Relevance

Bridges the testing-effect literature and professional/continuing education contexts. While written for a nursing audience, the principles generalise broadly. The paper's emphasis on self-testing as a life-long learning habit is particularly relevant for instructional designers and professional development programmes.
