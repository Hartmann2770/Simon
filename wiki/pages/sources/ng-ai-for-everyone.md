## Citation

Ng, A. (n.d.). *AI For Everyone*. Coursera / DeepLearning.AI. https://www.coursera.org/learn/ai-for-everyone

## Key Findings

1. A non-technical introduction to AI for business and organizational leaders. The core message: AI competency is necessary not just for engineers but for everyone involved in strategy, management, and product decisions.
2. **What AI can and cannot do**: well-defined tasks with clear input-output relationships and large training data (e.g., image classification, translation) are tractable for current AI; novel reasoning, contextual judgment, and tasks with few training examples remain difficult.
3. The AI project workflow is demystified: data collection, model training, iterative testing, deployment, and monitoring are presented as a systematic (but not necessarily technical) organizational capability.
4. **AI strategy for organizations**: successful AI adoption requires not just technical investment but data strategy, organizational change management, and building AI literacy across non-technical functions.
5. AI will transform most industries and roles, but the timeline and extent depend heavily on organizational readiness, data availability, and the specific tasks involved.

## Methodology

Online course. Video lectures and readings. Educational, not original research.

## Relevance

Foundational AI literacy resource. Useful for understanding how to situate AI-in-education tools within a broader organizational and strategic context. Relevant to L&D professionals who need to evaluate, commission, or use AI tools without deep technical expertise.
