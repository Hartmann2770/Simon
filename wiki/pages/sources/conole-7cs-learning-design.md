## Key Findings

1. The 7Cs of Learning Design is a holistic framework for designing technology-enhanced learning activities and entire courses, aiming to help educators make design decisions explicit and evidence-informed.
2. The seven Cs are: **Conceptualise** (define the learning vision and philosophy), **Capture** (identify relevant open educational resources and content), **Communicate** (plan how learners will interact with others), **Collaborate** (design collaborative activities), **Consider** (ensure reflection and metacognitive activities), **Combine** (map all elements into a coherent whole), and **Consolidate** (evaluate and refine the design).
3. The framework is iterative rather than linear; designers cycle through the Cs as they refine their design.
4. A key pedagogical commitment underlying the 7Cs is that learning design should be made *visible* and *shareable* — moving from tacit instructor craft to an explicit, communicable design practice.
5. The framework is particularly oriented toward technology-enhanced and distance learning contexts, providing tools for designers to think systematically about how technology mediates each of the seven dimensions.
6. An associated toolkit provides practical worksheets, storyboard templates, and course-view maps to operationalise the framework.

## Methodology

Framework paper and practitioner toolkit. Developed through iterative design research at the University of Leicester as part of the OER (Open Educational Resources) programme. Draws on learning design theory and Conole's prior work on e-learning frameworks.

## Relevance

One of the most comprehensive holistic frameworks for learning design, particularly in higher education and technology-enhanced contexts. Differs from CLT-derived frameworks by emphasising social, communicative, and reflective dimensions alongside cognitive load management.
