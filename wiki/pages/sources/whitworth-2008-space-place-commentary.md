## Citation

Whitworth, A. (2008). The organisation of space and place: A commentary on Wahlstedt et al. *British Journal of Educational Technology*, 39(6), 1031–1032.

## Key Findings

1. This brief commentary on Wahlstedt et al. (2008) argues that the space-to-place transition in e-learning depends not only on technical and social conditions but on **organizational structures** — the institutional norms, power relations, and design decisions that shape what kinds of social life are possible in e-learning environments.
2. Place-making in online learning is not a spontaneous social process but is afforded or constrained by institutional design: curricula that require collaboration create conditions for place; curricula organized around individual completion do not.
3. The commentary introduces the concept of **information architecture** as a key variable: how information is organized and presented shapes the social possibilities of a learning environment.

## Methodology

Brief commentary/response to Wahlstedt et al. (2008). Theoretical argument.

## Relevance

Extends Wahlstedt et al.'s space-to-place argument by foregrounding institutional and organizational dimensions. Useful for understanding that designing e-learning places requires attention to organizational context, not just technology.
