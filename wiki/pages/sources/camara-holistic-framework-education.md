## Citation

Camara, W. (n.d.). Beyond academics: A holistic framework for enhancing education and workplace success. [Journal article; full publication details unavailable in metadata.]

## Key Findings

1. Academic achievement alone is an insufficient predictor of either educational success or workplace performance; a holistic framework must incorporate non-cognitive competencies (attitudes, habits, social-emotional skills).
2. The framework proposes categories of competencies beyond academic knowledge: interpersonal skills, intrapersonal skills (self-regulation, resilience), critical thinking, and applied knowledge.
3. Learning design that focuses exclusively on content knowledge fails to develop the full range of competencies that education and employers require.
4. Assessment frameworks must similarly expand beyond recall-based testing to measure performance on complex, authentic tasks that engage the full competency profile.

## Methodology

Framework/conceptual paper. Limited metadata available; likely a synthesis drawing on educational assessment and workforce research.

## Relevance

Provides a rationale for expanding the scope of learning objectives and learning design beyond cognitive content. Complements Bloom's Taxonomy by foregrounding non-cognitive and affective competencies.
