# Report: XR Medical Device Training

## Citation

Maddox, Todd. "Report: XR Medical Device Training." *Tech Trends*. https://techtrends.tech/tech-trends/report-xr-medical-device-training/ (year not specified in metadata).

## Key Findings

1. Extended Reality (XR) — encompassing VR, AR, and mixed reality — is positioned as an ideal tool for accelerating and improving familiarisation with complex medical devices.
2. XR allows trainees to practice procedures on virtual devices without risk to patients, and without requiring access to physical equipment that may be expensive or scarce.
3. The report argues that XR can compress training time by providing focused, repeatable practice sessions that are not constrained by scheduling or resource availability.
4. Industry data cited indicates measurable improvements in proficiency and competency assessments among trainees who used XR-based device training versus traditional instruction-only approaches.
5. The report advocates for greater integration of XR into medical device regulatory training pathways.

## Methodology

Industry report / practitioner analysis. Not a peer-reviewed study. Based on synthesis of case studies and industry data. Author is a learning science practitioner affiliated with the XR training industry.

## Relevance

This industry perspective complements peer-reviewed findings on [virtual reality in education](../concepts/virtual-reality-in-education.md) and [simulation-based education](../concepts/simulation-based-education.md) by highlighting the practical and logistical case for XR in professional training. It represents a practitioner viewpoint on [immersive learning](../concepts/immersive-learning.md) and should be read alongside more rigorous studies such as [Jensen & Konradsen 2017](jensen-konradsen-2017.md) and [Katz et al. 2020](katz-et-al-2020.md).
