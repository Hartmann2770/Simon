# A Review of the Use of Virtual Reality Head-Mounted Displays in Education and Training

## Citation

Jensen, Lasse X., and Flemming Konradsen. "A Review of the Use of Virtual Reality Head-Mounted Displays in Education and Training." *Education and Information Technologies* 23, no. 4 (2017): 1515–1529. https://www.researchgate.net/publication/321290560

[Read PDF in Zotero](https://www.zotero.org/esaitch/items/7ZLJSF7C/attachment/2DRH5IRB/reader)

## Key Findings

1. Following a comprehensive literature search, 21 experimental studies were identified examining VR head-mounted displays (HMDs) in education and training contexts between 2013 and 2017.
2. Study quality was consistently below average on the Medical Education Research Study Quality Instrument (MERSQI), indicating methodological weaknesses in the field at that time.
3. HMDs were used across diverse domains: surgery, aviation, military, architecture, and general academic training.
4. Immersion and presence afforded by HMDs were associated with enhanced engagement and motivation, though their direct effect on learning outcomes was less clear.
5. Technical limitations (cybersickness, latency, cost, resolution) remained significant barriers to widespread adoption as of the review period.
6. The review identified a lack of standardised outcome measures, making cross-study comparison difficult.
7. There is a need for more rigorous controlled experiments to establish whether HMD-based VR produces learning advantages beyond conventional instruction.

## Methodology

Systematic literature review. Searched multiple databases for experimental studies published after 2012 using VR HMDs in educational or training contexts. Retrieved documents were quality-assessed using the MERSQI instrument. N = 21 studies included in final analysis.

## Relevance

This review provides the first systematic quality assessment of HMD-based VR in education during the post-2012 hardware generation (Oculus Rift era). It establishes a baseline for understanding the state of VR-in-education evidence before the field matured, and flags methodological standards that later work must address. Relevant to any evaluation of [virtual reality in education](../concepts/virtual-reality-in-education.md).
