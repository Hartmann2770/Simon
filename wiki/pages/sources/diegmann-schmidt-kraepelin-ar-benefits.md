# Benefits of Augmented Reality in Educational Environments: A Systematic Literature Review

## Citation

Diegmann, Phil, and Manuel Schmidt-Kraepelin. "Benefits of Augmented Reality in Educational Environments: A Systematic Literature Review." *(Journal and year not specified in metadata.)*

## Key Findings

1. Synthesising 25 studies, the review identifies a core set of benefits associated with AR in educational contexts: improved engagement, enhanced spatial understanding, and support for collaborative learning.
2. AR applications fall into distinct categories with different benefit profiles: marker-based AR (overlaying digital content on physical markers), location-based AR (GPS-triggered), and projection-based AR — each suited to different pedagogical purposes.
3. The most consistently reported benefit across studies is increased learner motivation and engagement, corroborating findings from experimental studies in the VR domain.
4. Learning outcomes (knowledge acquisition, skill development) showed positive effects in the majority of reviewed studies, though effect sizes and methodological rigour varied considerably.
5. Spatial understanding tasks — geometry, anatomy, architecture — benefited most strongly from AR, aligning with theories of spatial cognition and the specific affordance of overlaying 3D information in real space.
6. Collaborative affordances of AR (multiple users viewing the same augmented content) were underutilised in the reviewed literature, representing an opportunity for future design.

## Methodology

Systematic literature review. Searched multiple databases for peer-reviewed studies on AR in educational environments. Inclusion criteria: empirical evaluation of AR in educational or training contexts. 25 studies included; findings synthesised thematically by benefit category.
