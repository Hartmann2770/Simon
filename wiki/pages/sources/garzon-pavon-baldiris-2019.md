# Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis of Augmented Reality in Educational Settings

## Citation

Garzón, Juan, Juan Pavón, and Silvia Baldiris. "Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis of Augmented Reality in Educational Settings." *Virtual Reality* 23 (2019): 447–459. https://doi.org/10.1007/s10055-019-00379-9

[Read PDF in Zotero](https://www.zotero.org/esaitch/items/MQS3WPVV/attachment/KI2RTHAY/reader)

## Key Findings

1. Meta-analysis of 61 studies (2012–2018) found a statistically significant positive overall effect of AR on learning outcomes (standardised mean difference reported as moderate-to-large).
2. AR produced stronger effects in higher education than in K-12 settings, possibly because higher education tasks involve more abstract or complex content that benefits from spatial and 3D visualisation.
3. STEM disciplines (science, technology, engineering, mathematics) showed larger effect sizes than humanities or language domains.
4. The type of AR device moderated outcomes: head-mounted AR showed larger effects than tablet/smartphone AR, suggesting that immersion level matters even within the AR category.
5. Studies were often small in scale and short in duration; longer-term retention effects of AR are understudied.
6. The review identifies an underrepresentation of learners with special educational needs in the AR literature, flagging an equity gap.

## Methodology

Systematic review and meta-analysis. Searched Scopus, Web of Science, ERIC, and IEEE Xplore. Inclusion criteria: peer-reviewed empirical studies with quantitative learning outcome data. 61 studies included. Effect sizes calculated as Cohen's d; moderator analyses conducted for educational level, discipline, and device type.

## Relevance

The most rigorous quantitative synthesis in the AR-in-education literature in this wiki. The moderator finding that device type matters (HMD > tablet) bridges the VR and AR literatures, supporting a continuum-of-immersion argument also visible in [Krokos et al. 2019](krokos-plaisant-varshney-2019.md). The positive overall effect aligns with [Diegmann & Schmidt-Kraepelin](diegmann-schmidt-kraepelin-ar-benefits.md) and [Majeed & Ali 2020](majeed-ali-2020.md).
