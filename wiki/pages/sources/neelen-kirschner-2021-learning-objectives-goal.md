## Citation

Kirschner, P. A., & Neelen, M. (2021, July 20). Learning objectives: GOAL!?! *3-Star Learning Experiences*. https://3starlearningexperiences.wordpress.com/2021/07/20/learning-objectives-goal/

## Key Findings

1. The post argues that the most effective use of learning objectives is not as a content roadmap displayed at the start of a lesson but as *pre-test questions* posed before instruction.
2. Invoking Johan Cruyff's aphorism ("If you don't shoot, you can't score"), the authors argue that making learners attempt to answer the objective *before* they have been taught it creates productive struggle that primes encoding.
3. This is consistent with the *testing effect* and *pre-testing* literature: attempting to retrieve (or generate) answers before instruction improves subsequent encoding, even when initial answers are wrong.
4. Learning objectives stated as passive information ("By the end you will understand X") have no evidence base for improving learning; objectives used as active questions or prompts do have such a base.
5. The post critiques the reflexive inclusion of objectives-as-headers in corporate and academic instruction as an evidence-free practice.

## Methodology

Evidence-informed practitioner blog post drawing on learning science literature. Not an empirical study.

## Relevance

Interesting reframing of learning objectives as retrieval/pre-testing tools rather than content signposts. Connects objectives-design to the retrieval practice and testing effect literatures.
