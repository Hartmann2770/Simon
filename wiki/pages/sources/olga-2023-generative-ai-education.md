## Citation

Olga, T. (2023). Generative AI: Implications and applications for education. Preprint/report.

## Key Findings

1. The launch of ChatGPT in November 2022 precipitated both panic and enthusiasm among educators — the article attempts a balanced assessment of actual capabilities, limitations, and appropriate uses.
2. **Generative AI in education** has genuine promise for: personalized feedback on student writing, tutoring and explanation of complex concepts, scaffolding first drafts of creative work, and generating diverse assessment items.
3. Key risks include: academic integrity concerns (AI-generated work submitted as student's own), dependence effects reducing cognitive engagement, algorithmic bias in AI outputs, and misinformation from AI hallucination.
4. The article argues for educators to engage with generative AI rather than ban it: banning is both practically difficult and pedagogically counterproductive. Instead, curricula and assessments must be redesigned to be meaningful in an AI-available world.
5. **AI literacy** — the ability to critically evaluate, prompt effectively, and use AI ethically — should be treated as a new foundational competency for students.

## Methodology

Review and synthesis article. Analysis of available literature on generative AI and education combined with author's own experimentation with tools.

## Relevance

Accessible overview of the generative AI moment in education. Useful for contextualizing more specific empirical studies (Kestin/Miller, Lira/Rogers, Suriano/Plebe) and for identifying design principles for AI-integrated learning environments.
