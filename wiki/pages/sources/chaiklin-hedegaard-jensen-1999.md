## Citation

Chaiklin, Seth, Mariane Hedegaard, and Uffe Juul Jensen, eds. *Activity Theory and Social Practice: Cultural-Historical Approaches*. Aarhus University Press, 1999. ISBN 87-7288-811-3. (Proceedings of the International Society for Activity Theory and Cultural Research Congress.)

## Key Findings

1. Cultural-historical activity theory (CHAT) is presented as a coherent interdisciplinary framework for understanding human practice in social and institutional contexts.
2. The volume situates activity theory within a broad tradition spanning Vygotsky, Leont'ev, and subsequent generations of researchers in Europe and North America.
3. Social practice — the collective, culturally mediated doing of things — is the primary unit of analysis, not individual cognition.
4. Contributors demonstrate how activity theory illuminates transformation of practices across educational, professional, and community settings.
5. The framework insists on the unity of individual development and social-historical conditions: persons cannot be understood apart from the activity systems they participate in.

## Methodology

Edited conference proceedings gathering theoretical and empirical contributions from an international congress. Chapters vary in method — some are theoretical exegeses, others are qualitative case studies in workplace and educational settings.

## Relevance

This volume functions as a key reference compendium for activity theory's cultural-historical roots. It is particularly useful for understanding how Chaiklin and colleagues extend the Vygotskian tradition into contemporary educational and workplace research. For learning theory, the central insight is that learning is not a property of individuals but a transformation of participation in culturally structured activities.
