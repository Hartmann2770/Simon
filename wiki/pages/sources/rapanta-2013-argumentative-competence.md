## Citation

Rapanta, C., Garcia-Mila, M., & Gilabert, S. (2013). What is meant by argumentative competence? An integrative review of methods of analysis and assessment in education. *Review of Educational Research*, 83(4), 483–520.

## Key Findings

1. **Argumentative competence** is defined as the ability to produce, evaluate, and respond to arguments effectively — a complex multi-component skill that includes logical, rhetorical, and dialogic dimensions.
2. The review identifies three major traditions in argumentation research: **logical/structural** approaches (analyzing argument validity and inference structure), **rhetorical** approaches (analyzing persuasive effectiveness), and **dialogic/collaborative** approaches (analyzing how arguments function in conversational interaction).
3. Most educational assessments of argumentation focus on the structural quality of individual arguments rather than on dialogic competence — the ability to engage productively in argumentative dialogue with others.
4. Collaborative argumentation, in which groups work through disagreements to reach conclusions, produces more learning and better outcomes than individual argumentation or competitive debate — because it requires learners to genuinely engage with opposing views.
5. Methodological diversity across the literature makes it difficult to compare studies: different coding schemes, assessment instruments, and theoretical frameworks produce incompatible results.
6. Recommendations for educational practice: integrate collaborative argumentation tasks across subjects, not just in logic or rhetoric courses; teach both the structural and dialogic dimensions; provide extensive modeling and practice.

## Methodology

Integrative review. Systematic analysis of published methods for analyzing and assessing argumentation in educational settings. Covers multiple disciplines and methodological traditions.

## Relevance

Relevant to collaborative learning and higher-order thinking. Provides conceptual framework for understanding what argumentation competence is and how to assess it. Connects to AI-in-education (Suriano/Plebe on critical thinking) and collaborative learning research.
