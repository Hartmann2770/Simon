## Citation

Gazzard, A. (2011). Location, location, location: Collecting space and place in mobile media. *Convergence: The International Journal of Research into New Media Technologies*, 17(4), 405–417. https://doi.org/10.1177/1354856511414344

## Key Findings

1. Mobile media (location-aware games, augmented reality, geocaching) create new forms of spatial experience by layering digital information and game mechanics onto physical spaces — transforming familiar places into playful or informational environments.
2. The **collection mechanic** in mobile media (finding, tagging, or unlocking things at specific locations) encourages active physical exploration of space and creates new kinds of place-attachments through the activity of discovery.
3. Gazzard distinguishes between **traversal space** (moving through space as a navigational act) and **place** (space made meaningful through accumulation of experience and association). Mobile media can facilitate rapid place-formation through the intensity and specificity of location-based interaction.
4. Location-aware games demonstrate that digital information can transform the meaning of physical space without altering its physical properties — a street corner that is an ordinary location becomes a significant place when it is the site of a game event or geocache.
5. For education, location-based approaches can create powerful experiential learning: field trips augmented with AR, location-triggered information delivery, and place-based problem-solving challenges.

## Methodology

Theoretical and analytical. Draws on spatial theory (de Certeau, Tuan) and media studies frameworks, with analysis of specific mobile media examples.

## Relevance

Extends space/place theory to mobile and augmented reality media. Relevant to understanding how AR and location-based learning technologies create new forms of place-experience and to designing learning activities that leverage location.
