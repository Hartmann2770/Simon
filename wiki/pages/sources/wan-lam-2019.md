# The Effectiveness of Virtual Reality-Based Simulation in Health Professions Education Relating to Mental Illness

## Citation

Wan, Wai Hin, and Angie Ho Yan Lam. "The Effectiveness of Virtual Reality-Based Simulation in Health Professions Education Relating to Mental Illness: A Literature Review." *Health* 11, no. 6 (2019): 654–668. https://doi.org/10.4236/health.2019.116054

## Key Findings

1. VR-based simulation offers unique affordances for mental health education: learners can experience first-person simulations of psychosis, depression, and dementia, fostering empathy and perspective-taking that are difficult to achieve through conventional instruction.
2. The reviewed studies reported improved empathic attitudes toward patients with mental illness following VR simulation, particularly when learners adopted the patient's perspective.
3. Knowledge acquisition about mental illness symptoms and treatment was consistently improved by VR simulation compared to lecture or case-study controls.
4. VR's ability to provide a hazard-free environment with unlimited repetition is particularly valuable in health professions training, where real clinical exposure is limited and unpredictable.
5. Methodological quality of reviewed studies varied; most were small-scale and lacked long-term follow-up, limiting generalisability.
6. The review distinguishes VR simulation from high-fidelity mannequin simulation, arguing that VR's first-person perspective is a distinct and complementary affordance.

## Methodology

Narrative literature review. Searched PubMed, CINAHL, and PsycINFO for studies on VR-based simulation in mental health education. Focused on health professions students (nursing, medicine, social work). Extracted data on study design, outcomes, and quality.

## Relevance

This review positions VR as a tool for [simulation-based education](../concepts/simulation-based-education.md) in a domain where traditional simulation (mannequins, standardised patients) cannot replicate the subjective experience of illness. The empathy and perspective-taking outcomes are distinct from the cognitive knowledge outcomes discussed in [Jensen & Konradsen 2017](jensen-konradsen-2017.md). Relevant to both [virtual reality in education](../concepts/virtual-reality-in-education.md) and [immersive learning](../concepts/immersive-learning.md).
