## Citation

Suriano, R., & Plebe, A. (2025). Student interaction with ChatGPT can promote complex critical thinking skills. *Learning and Instruction*. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.learninstruc.2024.102011

## Key Findings

1. Students who engaged in structured dialogic interaction with ChatGPT showed improvements in **higher-order critical thinking** skills — specifically in argumentation, analysis of evidence, and evaluation of competing claims.
2. The interaction mode matters critically: passive use (asking ChatGPT for answers) did not improve critical thinking; **dialogic scaffolding** (being prompted to challenge ChatGPT's claims, identify weaknesses, and develop counter-arguments) produced the benefits.
3. The mechanism appears to be that challenging an AI interlocutor forces students to articulate and defend their reasoning in ways that rote learning or passive reading do not.
4. The study challenges the concern that AI interaction reduces students to passive information receivers; under the right conditions, AI can be an **intellectual sparring partner** that develops rather than atrophies critical thinking.
5. Design implication: educational AI tools should be explicitly designed to provoke student thinking, ask questions back, present alternative views, and require students to justify claims — not simply answer questions.

## Methodology

Quasi-experimental study with student participants. Compared conditions of passive AI use, structured dialogic AI interaction, and control (no AI). Critical thinking assessed with pre/post measures. Published in *Learning and Instruction* (peer-reviewed).

## Relevance

Adds nuance to the AI-in-education debate: shows conditions under which AI promotes rather than undermines complex thinking. Complements Lira & Rogers (coach vs. crutch) and Kestin & Miller (AI tutoring). Important for learning design of AI-mediated educational experiences.
