## Citation

Sugrue, B. (n.d.). A learning science alternative to Bloom's Taxonomy. *Learning Solutions Magazine*. https://learningsolutionsmag.com/articles/1116/a-learning-science-alternative-to-blooms-taxonomy

## Key Findings

1. Bloom's Taxonomy, while widely used, has significant limitations for instructional design: its six cognitive levels do not reliably map onto distinct instructional or assessment methods, and the hierarchy from recall to synthesis is not supported by cognitive science.
2. Sugrue proposes two alternatives. The first is based on Merrill's *content-performance matrix*, which classifies learning tasks by content type (fact, concept, procedure, principle) crossed with performance level (recall vs. use): this produces a design-actionable matrix rather than a linear hierarchy.
3. The second ("pure performance") alternative dispenses with content classification entirely and asks designers to specify only what the learner will *do* in the performance context — a fully behavioural formulation.
4. The key practical advantage of these alternatives is that they generate *different instructional methods* for different cell entries, whereas Bloom's levels do not reliably predict what teaching strategy is appropriate.
5. The article critiques Bloom's Taxonomy not as wrong but as *insufficient for design purposes* — it can describe levels of learning but does not prescribe how to achieve them.

## Methodology

Conceptual magazine article drawing on Sugrue's own prior research (2002) and Merrill's content-performance matrix. Not a systematic empirical study.

## Relevance

Important critique of Bloom's Taxonomy for instructional designers. Proposes actionable alternatives grounded in cognitive science of learning. Essential reading alongside the 2020 eLearning Coach article on alternatives to Bloom's.
