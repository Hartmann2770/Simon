## Citation

Rosenshine, B. (n.d.). *Principles of instruction*. International Academy of Education / UNESCO. (Reprinted from the 2010 Educational Practices Series.)

## Key Findings

The pamphlet presents essentially the same ten principles as the 2012 *American Educator* article but in a condensed booklet format intended for international distribution by UNESCO. Key points:

1. The pamphlet is the most widely circulated version of Rosenshine's principles internationally and is freely available from UNESCO; it is frequently cited alongside or instead of the journal version.
2. It emphasises that the principles are not a rigid teaching script but a research-informed repertoire that effective teachers deploy flexibly according to context.
3. Practical classroom examples are provided for each principle, making the pamphlet directly actionable by teachers without mediation by researchers.

## Methodology

Policy/practitioner pamphlet synthesising the same evidence base as the 2012 *American Educator* article. No new original data.

## Relevance

Important as the widely distributed practitioner reference for Rosenshine's principles. Often the version teachers encounter first. Cross-reference with the 2012 journal version for fuller elaboration.
