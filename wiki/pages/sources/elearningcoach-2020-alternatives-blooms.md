## Key Findings

1. The post surveys multiple frameworks proposed as alternatives or complements to Bloom's Taxonomy in workplace learning contexts, where the academic structure of Bloom's often maps poorly onto performance requirements.
2. Key alternatives reviewed include: Merrill's First Principles of Instruction, Gagné's conditions of learning (with nine events of instruction), and performance-outcome frameworks focused on what employees can *do* rather than what they *know*.
3. A common thread across alternatives is the shift from a recall-to-synthesis *hierarchy* to a *task-type* classification: different task types (motor skills, attitudes, verbal information, intellectual skills, cognitive strategies) require fundamentally different instructional conditions.
4. For workplace learning, the primary question is almost always *transfer to job performance*, which neither Bloom's recall nor comprehension levels address directly.
