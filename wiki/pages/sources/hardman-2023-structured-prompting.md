## Citation

Hardman, P. (2023). Structured prompting for educators. *Dr Phil's Newsletter, Powered by DOMS AI*. https://drphilippahardman.substack.com/

## Key Findings

1. Generic prompts produce generic outputs from LLMs; effective educational use of generative AI requires **structured prompting** — providing the AI with the pedagogical context, learner characteristics, learning objectives, and evidence-based design constraints it needs.
2. The author introduces a **CRAFT** prompting framework for educators: Context, Role, Action, Format, and Target audience. Each element shapes the AI's output to be more educationally appropriate.
3. Prompting is a skill that improves with practice and domain knowledge: educators with deep pedagogical expertise can direct AI tools to produce higher-quality learning content than non-experts, because they know what "good" looks like.
4. The major risk is that educators without strong learning-science grounding will use AI to produce content that looks pedagogically sophisticated (well-formatted, comprehensive) but is not evidence-aligned.
5. Hardman advocates treating prompt engineering as a professional competency for instructional designers and educators — not a technical skill but a pedagogical one.

## Methodology

Newsletter article / practitioner reflection. Based on experimentation with LLMs in instructional design workflow. Not a controlled study.

## Relevance

Practical guidance on AI-assisted instructional design. Relevant to prompt engineering as a professional skill and to understanding how AI tools need domain expertise to produce good learning content.
