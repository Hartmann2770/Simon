## Citation

Wang, S., & Zhang, H. (2026). Pedagogical partnerships with generative AI in higher education: How dual cognitive pathways paradoxically enable transformative learning. *International Journal of Educational Technology in Higher Education*, 23(1), 11. https://doi.org/10.1186/s41239-026-00585-x

[Read PDF in Zotero](https://www.zotero.org/esaitch/items/9IFB5S5P/attachment/QGWGT9ZJ/reader)

## Key Findings

1. **Dual cognitive pathways**: Students with a strong partnership orientation toward AI simultaneously activate two seemingly opposed pathways — *cognitive vigilance* (critical evaluation of AI output, β=0.335) and *cognitive offloading* (strategic delegation of cognitive tasks, β=0.351). Both independently predict transformative learning.
2. **The offloading paradox**: Contrary to the assumption that offloading erodes learning, strategic offloading — when it crosses a sufficient threshold — liberates mental resources for higher-order reflection, enhancing transformative learning. Partial, half-hearted offloading (Zone 2) produces *worse* outcomes than no AI use at all due to coordination overhead without freed capacity.
3. **Three zones of AI use**: Zone 1 (no AI, full manual load) produces slow but real learning; Zone 2 (scattered, partial AI use) produces worst outcomes; Zone 3 (committed, strategic offloading of entire task categories) produces dramatically improved learning.
4. **Efficiency orientation as amplifier**: Efficiency-seeking behaviour strengthens rather than undermines both vigilance and offloading pathways, acting through pragmatic risk management rather than shortcutting.
5. **Cross-cultural robustness**: Findings hold across China, Europe, and the United States (N=912), reinforcing generalizability.
6. **Reframing student–AI dynamics**: The study reconceptualizes the relationship from a zero-sum resource competition (AI does work → student learns less) to a synergistic collaboration (AI handles routine → student invests in depth).

## Methodology

Mixed-methods: three-wave time-lagged survey (N=912) across three cultural contexts, analysed via structural equation modeling, importance–performance map analysis (IPMA), fuzzy-set qualitative comparative analysis (fsQCA), and semi-structured interviews.

## Relevance

Provides the first large-scale empirical evidence for the "offloading paradox" — that strategic delegation to AI can enhance rather than diminish deep learning. Directly challenges the blanket cognitive-offloading-is-harmful narrative and provides actionable design thresholds. Essential counterpoint to Lodge and Loble (2026).
