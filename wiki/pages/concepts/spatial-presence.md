# Spatial Presence

## Definition

Spatial presence is the subjective sense of "being there" — the phenomenological experience of being physically located within a mediated environment. In VR and immersive media research, spatial presence is distinct from the technology's objective immersion level (the degree to which the system provides sensory information), though higher immersion typically affords higher presence. Presence is a psychological state, not a technical property. It is typically measured through self-report scales administered after immersive experiences.

## Key Principles

1. **Presence vs. immersion**: Immersion is a technical property of the system (field of view, resolution, head-tracking, haptic feedback). Presence is the psychological experience generated by that immersion. High-immersion systems tend to generate high presence, but the relationship is mediated by individual factors (attention, prior experience, content engagement).

2. **Spatial encoding advantage**: The brain evolved to encode information spatially — location, distance, orientation, and relative position are processed with high precision and durability. VR environments that generate high spatial presence leverage this capacity: information encountered in a spatially rich, tracked environment benefits from the same encoding mechanisms as information in real environments.

3. **Presence and autobiographical memory**: [Schöne et al. (2019)](../sources/schone-wessels-gruber-2019.md) demonstrated that higher spatial presence in VR correlates with VR experiences being encoded as genuine autobiographical memories — with the phenomenological richness (visual imagery, spatial context, emotional detail) characteristic of real episodic events.

4. **Presence as a mediator of VR learning outcomes**: [Cho](../sources/cho-spatial-presence-vr.md) found that spatial presence mediates the relationship between immersion level and learning outcomes — it is not the technology per se, but the sense of being in the learning environment, that predicts better retention and motivation.

5. **Presence in AR**: While AR does not replace the real world, it can generate a partial form of spatial presence by embedding virtual content within the learner's real environment. [Carmichael et al.](../sources/carmichael-biddle-mould-ar-learning.md) identify "spatial awareness" — the AR system's use of real space for content anchoring — as a key cognitive advantage.

## Evidence

**Autobiographical quality of VR memories**: [Schöne et al. (2019)](../sources/schone-wessels-gruber-2019.md) — VR experiences encoded with high presence show memory characteristics (vividness, detail, emotional tone) similar to real events rather than to media viewing.

**Recall advantage in virtual memory palaces**: [Krokos et al. (2019)](../sources/krokos-plaisant-varshney-2019.md) — HMD VR users (higher presence via head-tracking, vestibular cues) recalled 8.8% more items in a memory palace task than desktop VR users (lower presence). The proprioceptive and vestibular cues available in HMD VR are specifically cited as contributing to spatial encoding.

**Presence mediates retention and motivation**: [Cho](../sources/cho-spatial-presence-vr.md) — In second-language vocabulary learning, spatial presence scores predicted both delayed vocabulary retention and self-reported motivation to continue learning.

**Immersion level moderates AR outcomes**: [Garzón et al. (2019)](../sources/garzon-pavon-baldiris-2019.md) — The meta-analysis found that head-mounted AR produced larger learning effect sizes than smartphone AR, consistent with the hypothesis that higher spatial engagement (even within AR) produces better outcomes.

## Applications

- **Designing for presence**: Instructional designers working with VR should prioritise head-tracking, spatial audio, and interactive engagement to maximise presence — and thus learning benefits — rather than optimising only for visual quality.
- **Memory palace applications**: The method of loci is demonstrably more effective in high-presence VR environments; this has direct application for high-volume memorisation tasks (medical terminology, historical events, legal frameworks).
- **Evaluating immersive tools**: Presence should be measured as an intermediate outcome in VR/AR learning research — not just test scores — to understand the mechanism of observed effects.
- **AR content anchoring**: In AR design, spatially embedding content in the learner's actual environment (rather than displaying it in a floating overlay disconnected from context) enhances the spatial presence effect.
