## Definition

Interleaving (interleaved practice) is a study or practice arrangement in which different types of problems, concepts, or topics are mixed together within a single practice session — as opposed to blocked practice, in which all items of the same type are grouped together. Interleaving is considered a [desirable difficulty](desirable-difficulties.md): it makes practice harder and performance during practice appears worse, yet produces significantly better performance on final retention and transfer tests.

## Key Principles

1. **Interleaving forces discrimination.** When problems or concepts of the same type are blocked together, learners never need to identify *which* type they are dealing with — they know in advance. Interleaving removes this cue, forcing learners to actively identify the category of each new item and select the appropriate strategy. This additional cognitive demand produces deeper encoding and more robust learning.

2. **Interleaving promotes comparison and contrast.** Seeing different types of problems or examples in close succession allows learners to notice the features that distinguish them, building more precise category representations.

3. **Performance during practice is misleadingly poor.** Because blocked practice gives advance knowledge of problem type, it allows performance-based shortcuts. Interleaved practice prevents shortcuts, making learners' performance appear worse during training — but this surface decline masks deeper learning.

4. **The discrimination-learning account** (articulated by [Rohrer, 2012](../sources/rohrer-2012.md)): the core benefit of interleaving is not just harder retrieval but specifically the demand to classify and select among similar alternatives. This explains why interleaving is especially powerful when concepts or problem types resemble each other.

5. **Spacing and interleaving often co-occur.** When practice is interleaved across topics, items from the same category are necessarily separated in time — creating spacing. Some of the benefit attributed to interleaving may be due to embedded spacing, though research suggests both contribute independently.

## Evidence

[Rohrer (2012)](../sources/rohrer-2012.md) provides a review and theoretical account centred on the discrimination-learning mechanism. Multiple experiments comparing interleaved and blocked mathematics practice consistently favour interleaving on delayed final tests.

[Dunlosky et al. (2013)](../sources/dunlosky-et-al-2013.md) rated interleaved practice as "moderate" utility — less certain than practice testing or spacing due to fewer classroom-validity studies at the time, but promising. The principle aligns with the broader desirable-difficulties framework discussed in [Bjork (1994)](../sources/bjork-1994.md).

## Applications

- In mathematics, mix different problem types within homework sets rather than dedicating each assignment to a single procedure.
- In language learning, mix vocabulary from different semantic categories rather than studying one category at a time.
- In science education, interleave questions from different topics rather than covering one topic per assignment.
- In skill training, vary the type of exercise within sessions rather than drilling one skill to completion before starting another.
- Note: interleaving should be introduced gradually — for students with very low prior knowledge, some initial blocked practice may be necessary before interleaving becomes beneficial.
