About me

Learning Lead at Laerdal, working with how organizations design, scale, and measure learning in a world increasingly shaped by AI. The core of my work is helping teams turn advanced technology into practical capability, stronger decision‑making, and measurable performance, with learning impact as the primary measure of success.

With a background as a teacher and learning specialist, my work is grounded in a strong understanding of pedagogy and real‑world performance. Earlier in my career, I spent several years working hands‑on with XR, developing immersive learning solutions and design frameworks focused on achieving learning impact rather than novelty. That experience continues to shape how I approach new technology, with a clear bias toward deliberate design, productive effort, and skills that hold up in real situations.

Today, my work operates primarily at a strategic level, with a strong focus on AI. I work at the intersection of learning, technology, and human performance, helping organizations apply AI in ways that strengthen practice rather than shortcut it. A central concern in my work is ensuring that AI supports thinking, judgment, and readiness to act, especially in moments where confidence without competence is not enough.
